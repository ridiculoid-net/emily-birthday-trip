Isla Mujeres, Mexico
Tiny car-free island · 20-min ferry from Cancún
Best WalkabilityMost AffordableHurricane season
The BeachPlaya Norte — consistently one of the top beaches in the Caribbean. Waist-deep turquoise water, dead calm, almost no waves. Soft white sand.
Water Clarity⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ — electric turquoise, pristine visibility
Walkability to Food
The entire island is 5 miles long. Golf carts are the main transport. The main town center has excellent tacos, seafood, grocery stores, and beach bars all within a 5–10 min walk.
How Touristy?Day-trippers from Cancún arrive by 11am and leave by 4pm. Stay past sunset and it feels like a local Mexican island.
Getting ThereCHS → CUN (direct on Breeze, Spirit, or 1-stop via ATL/CLT)
Then: Ultramar Ferry from Puerto Juárez, ~20 min, $8–15 each way. Passport needed.
August WeatherWarm (~88°F), partly cloudy, occasional afternoon showers. Still beautiful beach weather.
Wildcard PerkSwim with whale sharks in late July–August — one of the most bucket-list experiences in the Caribbean.

Safest for AugustNo hurricane risk ✓More touristy

Exumas, Bahamas
Private islands & sandbars · Crystal-clear Caribbean
Most Remote & Pristine Untouched Beaches Hurricane season
The Beach Tropic of Cancer Beach — one of the most perfectly clear beaches on earth. Soft white sand, warm turquoise shallow water, almost no one around. Pig Beach (swimming pigs) is a short boat trip away.
Water Clarity ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ — arguably the best in the Caribbean. Iridescent turquoise, shallow sandbars, 30-ft visibility snorkeling
Walkability to Food
Georgetown / Farmer's Hill area has local restaurants and grocery stores. More remote Airbnbs may need a car. It's a small, quiet island — expect island-pace dining.
How Touristy? Very low. No large resorts dominate. The Exumas chain is mostly uninhabited cays. Day boats bring charter groups to Pig Beach, but the beaches are vast and rarely crowded.

06
Bonaire
Dutch Caribbean · Outside the hurricane belt · Diver's paradise
Safest Weather Outside Hurricane Belt Best Snorkeling
The Beach Bonaire Marine Park covers the entire island — the reef starts literally at the shore. 1,000 Steps, Bachelor's Beach, and Pink Beach are all stunning, calm, and crystal-clear.
Water Clarity ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ — world-class snorkeling and scuba diving. Protected reef with 60+ dive sites accessible from the beach. Sea turtles, rays, and vibrant coral everywhere.
Walkability to Food
Kralendijk is a small, walkable capital with excellent restaurants (Zeezicht, It Rains Fishes), waterfront bars, and a local supermarket. Very manageable on foot or bike.
How Touristy? Very low-key. No cruise ship port dominating. No huge resort strips. Attracts divers and eco-travelers. The island pace is genuinely relaxed — far less crowded than Aruba or Curaçao.
Getting There CHS → MIA or FLL → BON (Flamingo Airport)
United, American, or InselAir via Curaçao. 2 stops typically.
Passport required (Dutch Caribbean special municipality)
Pool Options Many Airbnbs have private pools or access to resort pool facilities. The Airbnbs below are all top-rated with pools — and the ocean is steps away from most.
August Weather Outside the hurricane belt (12°N latitude) — one of the few Caribbean islands statistically safe from hurricanes. Warm (~88°F), consistently sunny, steady trade winds keep it comfortable.
Wildcard Perk Rent a jeep and snorkel gear ($50–70/day) and explore 60+ shore dive sites — dno boat needed. Washington Slagbaai National Park has flamingo lagoons and deserted beaches. Truly unique.

07
Roatán, Honduras
Mesoamerican Barrier Reef · Most affordable · West Bay Beach
Most Affordable World-Class Reef Hurricane season
The Beach West Bay Beach — a stunning half-mile of white sand and turquoise water with the reef starting just offshore. Calm, clear, incredibly photogenic. West End is a short walk away with excellent restaurants and dive shops.
Water Clarity ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ — part of the Mesoamerican Barrier Reef (world's 2nd largest). Spectacular visibility, abundant marine life, snorkeling directly off the beach.
Walkability to Food
West End Village is a walkable strip with great seafood, local beach bars, dive shops, and small grocery stores. Very charming low-key Caribbean vibe.
How Touristy? Low-key. Popular with divers and backpackers, but no mega-resorts dominating the beach. West Bay has some busier stretches on cruise ship days — easily avoided. Most of the island is quiet.
Getting There CHS → RTB (Juan Manuel Gálvez Airport) via MIA, IAH, or ATL
American, United, or Copa Airlines · typically 1 stop
Passport required for Honduras
Pool Options Plenty of villas and casitas with private pools at affordable rates. The Airbnbs below offer pool + easy beach access — and Roatán is the most budget-friendly of all 7 destinations.
August Weather ~87°F, warm with some rain. In the hurricane belt but Honduras is rarely struck directly — trade winds and geography provide natural protection. Still, buy travel insurance.
Wildcard Perk World-class scuba diving at a fraction of the Caribbean average ($35–50/dive vs. $80+). Day trip to Cayos Cochinos (protected marine park, stunning) is a must.

08
Curaçao
Dutch Caribbean · Outside the hurricane belt · Cas Abao & Klein Curaçao
No hurricane risk ✓ ABC Islands Crystal Clear Water
The Beaches Cas Abao — consistently rated one of the Caribbean's most beautiful beaches: blinding white sand, turquoise water so clear you can see the bottom at 20 ft. Klein Curaçao is a deserted island with zero development and perfect snorkeling — reachable by day boat.
Water Clarity ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ — warm, impossibly clear, calm (sheltered from Atlantic swells). Coral reefs accessible directly from shore. Curaçao is regarded as one of the top shore-diving destinations on the planet.
Walkability to Food
Willemstad is a 15-min drive and one of the Caribbean's most charming cities — colorful Dutch colonial architecture, a stunning harbor, excellent restaurants, and a lively waterfront. Airbnbs near Jan Thiel Beach have beach bars and restaurants within walking distance.
How Touristy? Moderate. Willemstad gets cruise ships, but the beaches and resorts feel relaxed. Far less crowded than Aruba. Cas Abao Beach charges a small entry fee that naturally keeps crowds down.
Getting There CHS → CUR (Hato International Airport) via MIA
American Airlines or KLM · 1 stop · ~5h total
No visa required for US citizens
Accommodation Great mix of budget-friendly guesthouses near Jan Thiel, and luxury pool villas with ocean views. Curaçao offers better value than Aruba with equally beautiful water — a hidden gem for price-conscious travelers.
August Weather Outside the hurricane belt. Consistently warm (~88°F), sunny, steady trade winds keep it comfortable. One of the most reliably pleasant August-weather destinations in the Caribbean. Rarely rains significantly.
Wildcard Perk Colorful Willemstad (a UNESCO World Heritage Site) is unlike any other Caribbean city. Flamingos at Jan Kok Salt Flats, cave snorkeling at Mushroom Forest, and the floating Queen Emma pontoon bridge are all uniquely Curaçao.

09
Tobago
Trinidad & Tobago · Outside the hurricane belt · Pigeon Point Beach
No hurricane risk ✓ Pristine Reef Uncrowded
The Beach Pigeon Point Heritage Beach — the quintessential Caribbean postcard: a long sandy spit extending into brilliant turquoise water, thatched-roof huts, gentle calm waves. Englishman's Bay is equally stunning and far more secluded — one of the most photogenic beaches in the entire Caribbean.
Water Clarity ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ — Tobago sits at 11°N with warm, clear Caribbean water. Buccoo Reef (accessible by glass-bottom boat tour) and the offshore reefs have outstanding visibility and abundant marine life including manta rays.
Walkability to Food
Crown Point area has a good cluster of local restaurants, beach bars, and seafood spots within easy walking distance of the main beaches. Scarborough (20 min drive) has a lively market and more dining options.
How Touristy? Refreshingly low-key. Tobago gets far fewer international tourists than most Caribbean islands — it's popular with Trinidadians on weekends, but the beaches are rarely packed. No mega-resorts, no cruise ship hordes. Very genuine Caribbean atmosphere.
Getting There CHS → TAB (A.N.R. Robinson International) via MIA → POS (Port of Spain)
Caribbean Airlines or American · 2 stops · ~6–7h total
No visa required for US citizens
Accommodation Crown Point has excellent value villas and guesthouses near the beach. Many properties have private pools at very reasonable prices — Tobago is one of the most affordable Caribbean destinations for quality stays. Luxury eco-lodges in the rainforest side are spectacular.
August Weather Outside the hurricane belt at 11°N. Warm (~87°F), some tropical afternoon showers but generally sunny mornings. Tobago has a lush green landscape year-round — a little rain keeps it beautiful. Statistically very safe from hurricanes.
Wildcard Perk Tobago is one of the world's top manta ray diving spots — Speyside on the windward coast has regular manta encounters. The lush rainforest (oldest in the Western Hemisphere) and Argyle Waterfall make for amazing day trips away from the beach.

10
Los Roques, Venezuela
Venezuelan Archipelago · Outside the hurricane belt · The Caribbean's best-kept secret
No hurricane risk ✓ Most Pristine Water Charter access only
The Beaches Arguably the most jaw-dropping water in the entire Caribbean. Francisquí, Crasquí, and Madrisquí are uninhabited cays surrounded by water so brilliantly turquoise it looks digitally enhanced. Shallow sandbars extend hundreds of feet — wade into crystal water up to your knees for 50 meters. Zero crowds.
Water Clarity ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ — consistently cited as some of the clearest, most turquoise water in the entire Caribbean. Visibility 60+ ft, warm (~84°F), teeming with marine life. The tidal flats and sandbars are unique in the Caribbean.
Walkability to Food
You stay in Gran Roque village (the only inhabited island), a charming no-cars fishing village with colorful posadas (guesthouses), local restaurants, and seafood. Meals are typically included in posada packages. Days are spent on uninhabited cays — usually by boat with your posada.
How Touristy? Very low. The archipelago is a national park — no large hotels, no resorts, no chain restaurants. Only ~40 posadas (small guesthouses, 4–12 rooms) exist. Daily visitor numbers are capped. You share a cay with at most a handful of other travelers — or nobody at all.
Getting There CHS → MIA → CCS (Caracas) → LRV (Los Roques)
AA to Caracas, then charter (~35 min) to Gran Roque
⚠️ US State Dept: Level 3 – Reconsider Travel for Venezuela.
Los Roques itself is safe and regularly visited; transit through CCS is brief but requires planning.
Accommodation Posadas (boutique guesthouses) are the only option — typically 4–12 rooms, meals often included, boats to uninhabited cays arranged daily. Prices are all-in. Budget posadas $150–200/night, luxury eco-posadas $280–380/night. Book well in advance.
August Weather Outside the hurricane belt at 11°N. Hot and sunny (~88°F). Brief afternoon squalls are common but mornings are almost always clear. Calm, clear water with consistent trade winds. Statistically one of the safest Caribbean destinations for August weather.
Wildcard Perk This is the destination people who've been everywhere go when they want to be amazed. The sandbank "cayo" experience — wading on a white sand bar in the middle of a turquoise lagoon with nobody around — is genuinely unlike anywhere else in the Caribbean. Kitesurfing conditions are world-class.

Colombian Caribbean · Outside the hurricane belt · Sea of Seven Colors
No hurricane risk ✓ Sea of Seven Colors Walkable island
The Water San Andrés is famous for its "Sea of Seven Colors" — the unique combination of reef depth, sand, and seagrass creates an almost unreal gradient of turquoise shades visible from both sea and the island's hillsides. Johnny Cay (a tiny uninhabited island 15 min by boat) has perfectly calm, clear water and white sand.
Water Clarity ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ — warm, brilliant turquoise, very clear. The island sits atop a coral barrier reef. Snorkeling at El Cove, Morgan's Cave, and around Johnny Cay is excellent. Calm water on the western (leeward) side.
Walkability to Food
The town center (El Centro) is very walkable with great seafood restaurants, local beach bars, and the famous crab-and-coconut local cuisine. The island is only 12km long — you can bicycle or rent a golf cart to get everywhere in 20 min.
How Touristy? Popular with Colombians (it's a free-trade zone — duty-free shopping), but largely off the international radar. You'll hear a lot of Spanish, experience genuine Caribbean Raizal culture, and feel none of the US-tourist-resort vibe. Very authentic, very relaxed.
Getting There CHS → ADZ (Gustavo Rojas Pinilla Airport) via MIA → BOG (Bogotá)
American + Avianca or LATAM · ~7–8h total
US citizens need passport; Colombia entry is visa-free
Accommodation Excellent value for quality. The island has a range of boutique hotels and Airbnbs — sea-view rooms near the beach for $100–180/night, luxury ocean-view villas with pools for $220–330/night. Prices are very reasonable for the quality of experience.
August Weather Outside the hurricane belt at 12°N. Warm (~87°F), some tropical showers but reliable sunny periods. Trade winds provide natural cooling. Statistically very safe from hurricanes — one of Colombia's most visited August destinations.
Wildcard Perk San Andrés has a unique Caribbean-Colombian-Raizal culture unlike anywhere else — the local music (vallenato mixed with calypso), seafood (whole crab in coconut sauce), and the golf cart sunset loop around the island are experiences you won't find in any other Caribbean destination. Also: duty-free rum.
